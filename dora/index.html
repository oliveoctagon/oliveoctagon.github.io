Frequently Asked Questions

What does DORA stand for?

DORA stands for the Digital Operational Resilience Act, a regulatory framework designed to enhance the digital resilience and security of financial institutions and critical service providers within the EU.

What is the DORA RTS?

The DORA Regulatory Technical Standards (RTS) provide detailed technical and operational guidelines to ensure consistent implementation of DORA across the EU, clarifying the requirements for compliance.

Where can I download DORA?

DORA isn't a downloadable tool. It is a set of regulations published by the European Union. You can view the official documentation on websites such as the European Commission or the European Banking Authority.

How do I report the Register of Information under DORA?

Reporting under DORA typically involves gathering data on your IT systems and third-party arrangements, validating this data, and submitting it to your national regulator. Olive Octagon's tool simplifies this process by converting your Excel data into a fully compliant report.

What is the best way to comply with the new DORA regulations?

The most effective way to comply with DORA is to use an automated, expert-designed solution that streamlines data collection, validation, and report generation. Our tool is purpose-built to make this process quick and reliable.

Who is required to comply with DORA?

DORA primarily targets financial institutions—such as banks and insurance companies—but may also apply to other organizations that are critical to the financial system and rely heavily on digital operations.

How does Olive Octagon's DORA Reporting tool benefit my business?

Our tool converts your familiar Excel spreadsheets into validated, regulator-ready reports in minutes, reducing errors and saving time. This allows you to focus on your core business while staying compliant.

Is Olive Octagon's DORA Reporting tool secure?
